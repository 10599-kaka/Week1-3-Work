Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Energy Rating Label?
The Energy Rating Label shows how efficient an appliance is on a scale of 1 to 10+ stars. More stars mean lower running costs and better energy efficiency.
How much do appliances typically cost to run annually?
A standard fridge uses about 300–500 kWh per year. A 55-inch LED TV typically uses 100–200 kWh/year, while larger OLED models can use up to 250 kWh.
Do energy-efficient appliances really save money?
Yes. An 8-star rated appliance can save over $100–$200 per year compared to older 2–3 star models, depending on electricity prices and usage.
What is standby (vampire) power?
Standby power is electricity consumed by devices when they are turned off but still plugged in. It can account for 3–6% of a household’s total electricity bill in Australia.
